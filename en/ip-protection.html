Why you need it

Your idea was born before anyone else saw it. Can you prove it?

Copyright exists from the moment a work is created – but proving exactly when your work existed can be crucial in a dispute, a business negotiation, or when sharing a design with a client or investor.

Waipal helps you create a digital trail: an undeniable record that a specific file existed in a certain form at a certain time. The record is stored on the blockchain – a technology that ensures it cannot be retroactively altered or deleted.

Process

How intellectual property protection works

The whole process takes just a few minutes. No complex paperwork.

1

Upload your file

Select the document, photo, design, or other file you want to protect. Any format is supported.

2

Waipal creates a digital fingerprint

The system generates a unique digital fingerprint (hash) of your file. This fingerprint represents the exact form of the document.

3

The fingerprint is recorded on the blockchain

The digital fingerprint is written to the blockchain as an immutable timestamp. This step cannot be undone or altered retroactively.

4

You receive a certificate

You receive a certificate with all the information needed for verification – date, time, digital fingerprint, and blockchain identifier.

Service output

What the certificate contains and what it's used for

The certificate is a digital confirmation that a specific file existed in a certain form at a certain time. It can be verified at any time and presented as supporting evidence.

Date and time of record creation – precise timestamp of the blockchain entry

Digital fingerprint of the file – a unique hash that identifies the exact form of the document

Blockchain record identifier – a reference to the specific entry on the blockchain

File information – the name or description of the document entered during creation

Owner information – the name or entity of the person who created the certificate, including their identification detail (e-mail)

Verifiability – the certificate can be re-verified at any time to confirm its authenticity

The certificate can serve as supporting evidence when proving the existence, form, or ownership of a document. It is not a substitute for legal protection, but helps establish key facts.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions about IP protection

The certificate does not automatically create a legal claim to copyright. Copyright arises by law when a work is created. The certificate serves as supporting evidence of the existence of the work at a specific time and in a specific form, which can be very valuable when proving authorship.

Any modification to the file will change its digital fingerprint. The original certificate applies exclusively to the original version of the document. A new certificate must be created for a new version.

A certificate can be verified on the Waipal website by entering the identifier or uploading the original file. The system compares the digital fingerprint and confirms whether it matches the blockchain record.

Practically any digital file: documents (PDF, Word), images (JPG, PNG, SVG), videos, audio files, ZIP archives, and more. The file's content is not stored on the blockchain – only its digital fingerprint.
