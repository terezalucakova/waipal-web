How it works in practice

What encryption actually protects

Encryption is a technology that "locks" the content of a file – it converts it into a form that can only be read with the appropriate key. Without that key, the content is unreadable.

Protection in transit – data is encrypted during upload, not just after storage

Restricted access – the document can only be accessed via a verified account

Content protection – even if a data breach occurs, the content remains unreadable

Access control – you decide who has access to the document

Encryption does not replace a blockchain certificate – they are two different services. A certificate proves the existence of a document; encryption protects its content. Both services can be combined.
